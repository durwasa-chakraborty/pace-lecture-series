PACE Lecture Series: Introduction

Welcome to the PACE Lecture Series page! This platform is dedicated to cataloging monthly talks and lightning talks conducted by the members of the PACE Lab. The primary purpose of this page is to serve as a central repository for all past talks and their details.

Volunteering for a Talk

If you wish to volunteer for a talk, please contact the PACE Lab admin. The format for submitting your talk details and the related guidelines can be found here on the website. As the process becomes more streamlined, the format will evolve to ensure consistency.

Monthly Meets

The PACE Lecture Series hosts monthly meets to discuss and present research papers. The objective is to read one research paper per session, present its learnings, and share insights with the group. Please ensure that the paper you choose has been presented at top-tier conferences.

While the process aims to remain democratic, here are some principles inspired by Amazon that can guide the presentations:

  1. Avoid Slides for Scientific Talks
    Slides are excellent for product demos, marketing, or business discussions where overarching data drives decisions. However, scientific rigor demands detailed synthesis and in-depth analysis.

  2. Prepare a One-Page Summary
    Presenters are encouraged to prepare a concise one-pager that includes:

    • An overview of the paper.
    • Key learnings.
    • Experimental details.

    While it may seem counterintuitive to write another paper to explain an existing one, this approach helps simplify complex topics for a broader audience without assuming prior domain expertise.
